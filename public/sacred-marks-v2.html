Sacred Marks

One in four pregnancies end in loss. If you're here, you know this isn't just a statistic.

I've walked this path. Three times, my arms have been empty when they should have been full. Three times, I've felt the silence, the isolation, the desperate need for something—anything—to make the invisible visible.

I'm Jessica. I'm a Chinese medicine doctor, a fertility specialist, and a death doula. But before any of that professional training, I'm a bereaved mother who understands what it means when people don't know what to say. When your loss doesn't get acknowledged. When you need your baby's brief existence to matter.

This is why I do memorial tattoo work. Not as a business model, but as sacred work. Because I know what helps, and what doesn't. Because I know that sometimes the only thing that makes sense is a permanent mark for a permanent hole in your heart.

What I Offer

This isn't about pretty tattoos (though they are beautiful). This is about making your baby real to a world that wanted to move on too quickly. This is about having something you can touch when the grief hits. This is about not being alone.

  • Pregnancy Loss & Miscarriage Memorial — For those of us who lost babies the world never saw. Simple symbols, dates that matter, permanent proof they existed.
  • Stillbirth & Infant Death Memorial — Using actual footprints, handprints, names. Honoring babies who were here, however briefly.
  • Failed IVF & Fertility Journey Memorial — For embryos that didn't make it, treatments that didn't work, the version of yourself you thought you'd be.
  • Rainbow Baby Honoring — When joy and grief live together. Holding space for both your angel baby and the one in your arms.
"Your baby's story deserves to be told. Let me help you tell it."

How This Works

First, we talk. Free consultation, no pressure, just space to share your story. I'll ask about your baby, your loss, what you need this tattoo to do for you. Sometimes people are ready right away. Sometimes they need time. Both are okay.

I work with hand-poke (stick-and-poke) method. It's gentler, heals faster, and honestly, it feels more intentional. Less like getting tattooed, more like ceremony. The sessions are private. You can bring your partner, a friend, whoever you need. We can be silent or you can talk about your baby the whole time.

I combine this with my training in Chinese medicine (therapeutic acupoint placement), death doula work (holding space for grief), and my own experience of loss. This isn't transactional. This is witnessing.

Sliding scale pricing. Payment plans. I will never turn someone away because they can't afford it.

What People Say

(I'm just beginning this work publicly, but here's what I know from my own experience and from holding space with other bereaved parents:)

Memorial tattoos don't fix grief. They don't bring babies back. But they do something important: they make the loss visible. They give you something to touch when you need to feel close. They tell the world "this happened, this mattered, this baby is mine."

Research shows memorial tattoos help with continuing bonds—staying connected to your baby in a new way. They reduce isolation. They give you back some control in a situation where you had none.

Mostly, they just help. And when you're drowning in grief, "helps" is enough.
