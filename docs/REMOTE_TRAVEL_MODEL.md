**Practical Considerations**
- Portable tattoo setup (stick-and-poke ideal - minimal equipment)

## Mobile Tattoo Regulations

### Regulatory Landscape (2024)

**Three Categories of States**

**1. Prohibited (Cannot Do Mobile Tattooing)**
- Maine: Explicitly prohibits mobile tattoo facilities
- Likely others with strict brick-and-mortar requirements
- **Strategy**: Skip these states entirely for tattoo work

**2. Temporary Permit States**
- Kentucky: Mobile studios can operate 7 days per location within 90-day period
- Louisville: Cannot remain at one location >14 consecutive days
- Georgia: All body artists must have DPH certification (as of Oct 2024)
- **Strategy**: Obtain temporary permits for planned visits

**3. Permissive or Unclear**
- Many states don't specifically address mobile tattooing
- Some counties/cities regulate, not states
- Often depends on how strictly "studio" is defined
- **Strategy**: Contact local health departments, request guidance in writing

### Universal Requirements (Regardless of Location)

**Training & Certification**
- Bloodborne pathogen training (annual)
- CPR/First Aid certification
- Age 18+
- May need written/practical exam

**Equipment & Supplies**
- Sterile single-use needles
- Autoclave (for non-disposable equipment) OR all-disposable setup
- Sharps disposal container
- Medical-grade cleaning supplies
- Proper lighting
- Clean workspace

**Documentation**
- Liability insurance (travel-friendly policy)
- Client consent forms
- Medical history forms
- Photo documentation
- Appointment records

### Mobile Setup Options

**Option 1: Work from Client's Home**
- Bring portable setup
- Client provides clean, private space
- Requires health department approval (varies)
- Insurance considerations (some policies exclude this)

**Option 2: Rent Studio Space**
- Short-term rental of licensed tattoo studio
- Studio already has health department approval
- You work as "guest artist"
- Most compliant option
- Network with studios in cities you visit

**Option 3: Rent Commercial Space**
- Airbnb, hotel suite, rented office
- May need temporary health permit
- Check local regulations carefully
- Document cleanliness and setup

**Option 4: Mobile Unit (Van/Trailer)**
- Some states allow (see regulations)
- Requires vehicle setup approval
- Higher initial investment
- Ultimate flexibility if legal

### Stick-and-Poke Advantages for Mobile Work

**Why Hand-Poke Is Ideal for Travel**
- No tattoo machine = less equipment
- Quieter (can work in residential settings)
- Less intimidating for therapeutic clients
- Fits healing/ritualistic approach
- Easier to transport
- Lower barrier to entry

**Equipment List (Fits in Backpack)**
- Sterile needles (disposable)
- Tattoo ink (small bottles)
- Transfer paper/stencil materials
- Gloves (box)
- Cleaning supplies (small bottles)
- Sharps container (small, portable)
- Vaseline, ointment
- Paper towels, plastic wrap
- Headlamp/clip light (lighting)
- Portable table covering

## Digital Service Delivery

### What CAN Be Done Remotely (2024 Research)

**Death/Bereavement Doula Services**
- The Miscarriage Doula (founded 2020): Fully virtual bereavement doula services
- Proven effective for pregnancy loss support
- Video calls, messaging support, resource provision
- No state licensing barriers (doula field mostly unregulated)

**Birth Doula Services**
- Virtual doula care shows comparable outcomes to in-person (2024 study)
- 20% reduction in C-sections with 2+ virtual appointments
- California Medicaid: Allows all doula visits via telehealth (including labor/delivery prep)
- New York, Minnesota Medicaid: Specify doula services can be telehealth
- Labor/delivery must be in-person (but that's not tattoo work anyway)

**Acupuncture/TCM Consultations**
- Diagnostic consultations can be virtual (pulse and tongue via video)
- Herbal medicine consultations and prescriptions
- Dietary and lifestyle recommendations
- Point location education
- Ear seed instruction and mailing
- Cannot: Insert needles remotely (obviously)

**Design & Consultation**
- Full design process can be virtual
- Digital mock-ups, revisions
- Symbolism discussion
- Placement planning (client sends photos)
- Meaning-making conversations
- Grief support integration

### Remote Service Packages

- Months of remote doula support (birth or death)

- **Client pays**: Virtual services monthly + tattoo/travel fee

- Home base in tattoo-friendly state (register/license here)
- 2-3 anchor cities you visit quarterly (develop client base)
- Ad-hoc travel for special projects (Legacy Ink families, intensives)

**Example Schedule**
- **Month 1**: Home base (Portland, OR) - appointments, virtual clients, admin
- **Month 2**: Travel Circuit 1 - Seattle (1 week), San Francisco (1 week), LA (1 week)
- **Month 3**: Home base
- **Month 4**: Travel Circuit 2 - Denver (1 week), Santa Fe (1 week), Austin (1 week)
- **Months 5-6**: Home base + ad-hoc travel for special projects

**Client Development**
- Announce visits 4-6 weeks in advance
- Book appointments via website
- Return to same cities quarterly (build ongoing relationships)
- Virtual support between visits
- Intensive appointments during visit days

**Revenue**
- Consistent monthly income from virtual services
- Concentrated earnings during travel weeks
- Premium pricing for travel appointments
- Lower overhead (no permanent studio lease)

**Specialty (10% of business - As Desired)**

7. **Training/Education** - Teaching other practitioners

### Legal/Regulatory Strategy

**Step 1: Establish Home Base**
- Choose tattoo-friendly state for primary registration
- Get all required certifications and licenses
- Establish business entity, insurance, bank accounts

**Research-Friendly States:**
- Oregon, California, Colorado, New Mexico, Texas (research specific county/city regs)
- Avoid: Maine (prohibits mobile), strict Northeast states

**Step 2: Research Target Cities**
- Identify 6-8 cities you'd like to visit regularly
- Contact health departments in each
- Get guidance on temporary/mobile tattooing in writing
- Prioritize permissive jurisdictions

**Step 3: Guest Artist Relationships**
- Network with established studios in target cities
- Negotiate guest artist arrangements
- Work from their licensed space (easiest compliance)
- Build relationships over time

**Step 4: Expand Methodically**
- Don't try to work everywhere
- Build circuit of compliant locations
- Document everything
- Stay current on regulatory changes
